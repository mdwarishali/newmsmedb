Fixed Asset Register (FAR)

Fixed Asset Register (FAR) is invaluable. It provides a detailed record of all the fixed assets owned by a company. including their original purchase prices, dates of acquisition, locations, and current status. This information is essential for:

Accurate Valuation:

Ensuring that assets are correctly valued, which is critical for determining the amount of coverage needed and the appropriate settlement in the event of a claim.

Proof of Ownership:

Establishing ownership and the existence of assets, which is crucial for substantiating claims and avoiding disputes with insurers.

Damage Assessment:

Facilitating the assessment of damage or loss, as the FAR helps insurers and adjusters quickly identify the assets involved and their pre-incident condition.

The Reinstatement Value (RIV)

The Reinstatement Value (RIV) is crucial in insurance claims as it determines the cost to replace or repair an asset to its original condition at current prices. Its importance lies in:

Full Coverage:

Ensuring the insurance payout covers the actual cost of reinstating the asset, not just its depreciated value.

Asset Valuation:

Asset Valuation: Asset Valuation is the process of determining the reinstatement value of an asset.
